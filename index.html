Key Terms and How They Relate to the Dark Web

  • Bit: Encrypted messages on the dark web are built from bits — the 0s and 1s that carry hidden information across Tor networks.
  • Byte: Files and stolen data exchanged on the dark web are measured in bytes, the building blocks of all digital content.
  • Roundoff: Cryptography on the dark web relies on precision — even tiny roundoff errors can weaken encryption and compromise anonymity.
  • Analog Data: Voice and video (originally analog) are digitized before being transferred through dark web channels to maintain secrecy.
  • Lossless: Dark web users use lossless compression to share sensitive files like stolen databases without losing any details.
  • Lossy: Media shared on the dark web, like images or pirated videos, is often lossy-compressed for faster uploads and downloads.
  • Metadata: Metadata can expose identities or locations. On the dark web, stripping metadata is critical for staying anonymous.
