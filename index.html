What is the Dark Web?

The dark web is a hidden part of the internet that cannot be accessed through normal browsers like Chrome or Safari. Instead, special software such as Tor is needed. While some people use it for privacy and free speech, it is also home to illegal activities like drug markets, cybercrime, and stolen data trading.

Risks and Challenges

  • Exposure to scams, fraud, and malware.
  • High risk of accidentally interacting with illegal content.
  • Difficult to trust others due to lack of regulation.
  • Law enforcement often monitors dark web activity.

Cybersecurity threats are dangers that can harm computers, networks, and personal data. These include viruses, phishing scams, and DDoS attacks. On the dark web, hackers often sell stolen information, making cybersecurity more important than ever.

Conclusion

The dark web shows the balance between privacy and danger. While it can protect free speech, the risks of scams, cybercrime, and illegal activities make it a space that should be approached with extreme caution.
